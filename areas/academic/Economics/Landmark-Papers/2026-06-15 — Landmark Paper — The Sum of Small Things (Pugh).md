# The Sum of Small Things: A Theory of the Aspirational Class

**Author**: Allison J. Pugh  
**Year**: 2015  
**Citations**: ~350+  
**Venue**: Princeton University Press

## Key Contribution

Sociological economics of consumption: status anxiety drives consumption patterns across all income levels. "Aspirational class" concept — individuals aspire upward, consuming goods signaling aspiration rather than current class position. Explains consumption inequality and precarity among middle-class households.

## Intellectual Impact

Bridged economics and sociology; cited in consumer behavior, inequality, and status studies. Informed discussions on lifestyle inflation, debt, and precarity. Complementary to behavioral economics literature.
