# Trade and Inequality: From Theory to Estimation

**Authors**: Gene M. Grossman et al.  
**Year**: 2017  
**Citations**: ~580+  
**Venue**: *Review of Economic Studies*

## Key Contribution

Theoretical and empirical framework for linking trade exposure to wage inequality. Shows heterogeneous worker effects: trade harms some (import-competing) workers more than others (export-benefiting). Integrates firm heterogeneity, worker skill, and task specialization.

## Intellectual Impact

Refined trade-labor nexus literature; informed policy debates on trade's distributional consequences. Cross-citations in trade, labor, and political economy of globalization. Grounded arguments about losers from trade liberalization.
