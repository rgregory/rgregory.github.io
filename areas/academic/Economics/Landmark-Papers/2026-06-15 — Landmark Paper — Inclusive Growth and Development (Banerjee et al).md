# Inclusive Growth and Development

**Authors**: Abhijit Banerjee et al. / World Bank  
**Year**: 2015–2016  
**Citations**: ~1,800+  
**Venue**: World Bank policy reports + peer-reviewed publications

## Key Contribution

Framework linking inequality and growth: rapid growth with rising inequality may be unsustainable (political instability, social risk); "inclusive growth" emphasizes distributional concerns alongside aggregate expansion. Informs development policy priorities — education, institutions, safety nets, progressive taxation.

## Intellectual Impact

Shaped World Bank and IMF policy orientation post-2008. Cross-domain: cited in development economics, sociology, political economy, policy. Bridged gap between growth maximization and distributional concerns.
