# Xenotransplantation Progress (2025)

## Discovery
Significant clinical advances in using donor organs from pigs for human transplant recipients (xenotransplantation).

## Context
Global organ shortage:
- ~100,000+ patients on transplant waiting lists (US)
- Many die waiting
- Demand far exceeds human donor supply

## Xenotransplantation Approach
Use genetically modified pigs as organ donors:
- Xenogeneic organs (across species)
- Genetic modifications reduce immune rejection
- Pigs chosen for size compatibility and organ similarity

## 2025 Breakthroughs
- Improved immunosuppression protocols
- Better organ preservation and transport
- Increased graft survival times
- Reduced acute rejection episodes

## Organs Being Tested
- Heart
- Kidney
- Liver
- Pancreas

## Challenges
- Chronic rejection (long-term graft failure)
- Infectious disease risks (porcine retroviruses)
- Ethical considerations (animal welfare, naturalness)
- Regulatory pathways (FDA approval process)

## Clinical Potential
If successful at scale:
- Eliminates organ waiting lists
- Provides unlimited supply
- Improves patient outcomes and mortality
- Reduces healthcare costs
