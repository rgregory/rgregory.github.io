# How Nanobots Generate Energy

Nanobot energy generation varies by design and intended function. Key strategies:

## 1. Harvesting Ambient Energy
Nanobots can harvest energy from their environment:
- **Solar**: photovoltaic conversion if exposed to light
- **Thermoelectric**: converting thermal gradients into electrical energy
- **Kinetic**: harvesting mechanical energy from movement
- **Chemical**: energy from ambient chemical reactions

## 2. Biological Metabolism
Some nanobots are modeled on biological organisms and use biochemical reactions — for example, enzyme-catalyzed reactions that produce **ATP** (adenosine triphosphate), the cell's primary energy carrier. This mirrors how living cells generate energy.

See: [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Biology/2026-04-04 — Extracellular Digestion Absorptive Nutrition in Fungi|Extracellular Digestion]] for a parallel — fungi also exploit external biochemical reactions for energy.

## 3. External Power Sources
Nanobots can be powered by batteries or external power supplies — common when they operate as part of a larger device system.

## 4. Energy Storage
Advanced designs incorporate supercapacitors or micro-batteries within the nanobot structure, charged externally before deployment.
