# Being and Event — Alain Badiou (1988)

*L'Être et l'événement* — Badiou's magnum opus. Central ambition: rebuild ontology on ZF set theory, while developing a theory of rupture, novelty, and truth that set theory alone cannot contain.

---

## Central Thesis

**Ontology is mathematics. Mathematics is the science of being qua being.**

Being = pure multiplicity without unity. A set, stripped of interpretation, is just a multiple — a collection with no intrinsic properties beyond membership structure. The classical question "what is being?" is answered: being is what set theory talks about. Every other discourse talks about *beings*. The structure of situations, at the most fundamental level, is set-theoretic.

---

## Key Concepts

### The Multiple and the One

Multiplicity is primary; unity is always an operation performed on multiplicities. A "one" is never found — it is always *counted-as-one*. The operation of **presentation** takes a multiple and presents it as unified. The multiple precedes the count.

The **void (∅)** is fundamental — all sets are built from ∅. Being at its most basic is the void.

### Situation and State

- **Situation**: any presented multiplicity — a society, a field of knowledge, a historical moment
- **State of a situation**: the meta-structure counting *subsets* — the power set operation applied to the situation. Institutions, classifications, power.
- Cantor's theorem: $|\mathcal{P}(A)| > |A|$ — the state is always strictly larger than the situation.
- Political implication: the gap between situation and state — between what exists and what is officially recognized — is where politics lives.

### Evental Site

Elements of a situation that are presented but whose own elements are *not* presented — their interior is invisible to the situation's count. On the edge of the void. Where events happen.

### The Event

A supplement to the situation, occurring at an evental site, which includes itself in its own presentation. Self-referential. *Undecidable* from within the situation's knowledge. Cannot be recognized or predicted from the existing framework.

Examples: French Revolution, Cantor's invention of set theory, Schoenberg's twelve-tone music, Paul on the road to Damascus.

The event is not a fact — it is a rupture that creates the possibility of new facts.

### Intervention and Fidelity

After an event, a **subject** is constituted through **fidelity** — ongoing commitment to tracing the event's consequences through the situation. The event produces the subject. Fidelity is an infinite procedure: interrogating elements, asking "does this connect to the event?"

### The Generic and Truth

A **truth** is a **generic set** — assembled through fidelity, infinite, and corresponding to no definable predicate within the situation's knowledge. Uses Paul Cohen's **forcing** (1963) — the same technique proving the independence of the Continuum Hypothesis. A truth is to the situation what a generic extension is to a model of set theory. Truths are **indiscernible** — they exceed all classification.

**Knowledge vs. Truth**: Knowledge classifies and names. Truth is what exceeds all classification.

## Four Truth Procedures

| Domain | Event type | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Science | Mathematical/conceptual rupture | Cantor, Galileo, Einstein |
| Art | Artistic rupture | Schoenberg, Mallarmé, Celan |
| Politics | Emancipatory rupture | French Revolution, 1917, 1968 |
| Love | Encounter between two | The Two as truth procedure |

Philosophy does not produce truths — it *compossibilizes* them.

| Power Set | The state always exceeds the situation |

---

## Being vs. Event

- **Being**: consistent, regulated, well-founded multiplicity — what ZF describes
- **The Event**: self-belonging, undecidable, anti-foundational — what ZF *cannot* describe

The Axiom of Foundation forbids self-belonging. An event violates Foundation. Therefore: **ontology cannot account for the event**. The event is not a kind of being — it is a rupture *in* being.

---

## Critical Objections

1. **On the mathematical analogy**: Why is set theory *ontology* rather than a useful model? Asserted, not argued.
2. **On the event**: Non-well-founded structure sits outside standard ZF — coherent position or sleight of hand?
3. **On politics**: Fidelity to Maoism; the theory of fidelity can justify continued commitment to destructive projects.
4. **On love**: Most original and least contested part of the system.

---

## Why It Matters

1. Takes mathematics seriously as literal discourse of being — not analogy
2. Rehabilitates subject and truth against poststructuralist dissolution
3. Connects Cantor/Gödel/Cohen to political philosophy
4. Is a *system* — rare after decades of anti-systematic thought
