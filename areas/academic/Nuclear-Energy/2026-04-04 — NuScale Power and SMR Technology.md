# NuScale Power and SMR Technology

## NuScale Power — Overview

**Company:** Founded 2007, spun out of Oregon State University research. Backed by Fluor Corporation. Publicly traded (NYSE: SMR).

**The Product — NuScale Power Module (NPM):**
- Integrated pressurized water reactor (PWR) — same physics as conventional nuclear
- ~76 ft tall, 15 ft diameter — self-contained reactor + steam generator + pressurizer in one unit
- **77 MWe per module** (uprated from original 50 MWe)
- Submerged in underground water pool
- No large external coolant piping

**VOYGR Plant Configurations:**
| Config | Modules | Output |
|--------|---------|--------|
| VOYGR-4 | 4 | ~308 MWe |
| VOYGR-6 | 6 | ~462 MWe |
| VOYGR-12 | 12 | ~924 MWe |

**Passive Safety:**
- Decay heat removed by natural circulation — no pumps, no AC power, no operator action required
- Claims indefinite safe cooling without intervention (key lesson from Fukushima)
- No large-break LOCA scenario (integrated design eliminates large coolant pipes)

**Regulatory Milestones:**
- 2020: NRC Final Safety Evaluation Report — first SMR ever
- 2022: NRC Design Certification — first SMR certified in the U.S.
- 2023: Standard Design Approval for uprated 77 MWe design

**The UAMPS Collapse (2023):**
- Flagship project: 6-module plant at Idaho National Laboratory with Utah municipal utilities
- DOE committed $1.4B+ in support
- Projected LCOE ballooned from ~$58/MWh → ~$89–119/MWh
- Utilities dropped subscriptions; project cancelled November 2023
- NuScale laid off ~40% of staff

**Current Status:**
- Pursuing international markets: Romania (most advanced), Poland, others
- No construction contract exists anywhere
- Competitors closing the gap: GE Hitachi BWRX-300 has a committed build in Canada; TerraPower Natrium under construction in Wyoming

**Core Tension:** NuScale holds the most advanced U.S. regulatory position of any SMR — but licensing didn't translate to construction, and economics remain the unresolved challenge.
