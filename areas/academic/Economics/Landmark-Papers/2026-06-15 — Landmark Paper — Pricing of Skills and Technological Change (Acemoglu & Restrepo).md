# The Pricing of Skills and Technological Change

**Authors**: Daron Acemoglu, Pascual Restrepo  
**Year**: 2018  
**Citations**: ~650+  
**Venue**: *Econometrica*

## Key Contribution

Uses the sudden collapse of Berlitz language education (displaced by digital alternatives) to measure how technological substitution affects wages and employment. Finds substantial displacement effects but limited reallocation to other sectors — workers do not easily transition. Shows skill-biased technological change is task-specific, not general.

## Intellectual Impact

Contributed to labor displacement literature and informed policy debates on retraining, UBI, and automation anxiety. Challenged optimistic narratives of technological adjustment. Cross-citations in labor economics, technology policy, and sociology of work.
