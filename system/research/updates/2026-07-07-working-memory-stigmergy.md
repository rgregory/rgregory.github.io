## New findings

- The strongest new bridge is **salience-gated action selection**. Parr and Friston formalize working memory/attention/salience in active inference, while Ponzi models working memory as recurrent reactivation of salient cues that select and reinforce action. These sources make the “working memory as internal directive salience” reading more precise.
- The strongest ant-side bridge is **composite information use**. Czaczkes et al. show ants can combine private memory and pheromone/social information rather than acting as pure pheromone-following reflexes. This refines the analogy: pheromone cues are not simply external commands; they interact with individual memory and local state.
- The unconscious-working-memory literature complicates the thesis in a useful way. Gambarota et al. and the 2026 multisite registered report suggest some working-memory-like retention may occur without full conscious access, but the effect is small/controversial. This supports a graded continuum from nonconscious directive states to globally broadcast conscious contents.
- Global neuronal workspace theory still points to an important discontinuity: human conscious working memory is not merely a local directive cue; it becomes globally available through ignition/broadcast across processors.

## Evidence for the analogy

- Both working memory and pheromone systems can be modeled as **transient state variables that bias near-term action**.
- Both can work without a homunculus: ant trail following and neural salience gating rely on local mechanisms and feedback, not central command.
- Both appear sensitive to competition and displacement: working memory has capacity/interference limits; pheromone trails can be amplified, repelled, evaporated, or overridden by private information.
- Active inference gives a shared vocabulary: salience, precision, affordance, and action selection can describe internal neural control and external environmental cueing at different scales.

## Evidence against or limits

- **Substrate and access differ.** Working memory is an internal neural process; ant pheromone guidance is usually external/environmental and socially shared.
- **Conscious broadcast differs.** GNW theory suggests conscious working-memory contents gain global accessibility inside an organism; pheromone trails do not obviously create a unified first-person workspace for an ant or colony.
- **Ant behavior is not just pheromone behavior.** Ants combine pheromone cues with private memory and context. Treating pheromones as the whole decision system would be too simple.
- **Stigmergy may be broader than chemistry.** Termite construction research shows topological/environmental cues can guide distributed behavior; the better comparison may be “shared directive substrate” rather than specifically “pheromone.”
- **Invertebrate consciousness remains contested.** Nieder’s review treats working memory and voluntary attention as indicators of consciousness but finds limited evidence for conscious processing in invertebrates, with honeybees as a possible exception. That cautions against equating ant chemical guidance with conscious working memory.

## Concrete next questions

1. Can “directive salience system” be formalized as a common control pattern with slots for substrate, timescale, capacity, scope, and broadcast depth?
2. Does GNW-style global broadcast transform a directive salience state into conscious content, or merely make it reportable and integrative?
3. Are ant private memories and pheromone trails better understood as two coupled working memories: one internal-individual, one external-collective?
4. What experiments distinguish “unconscious working memory” from priming, iconic memory, or residual awareness?
5. Would active-inference terms such as precision weighting and epistemic affordance map cleanly onto pheromone strength, evaporation, and repellent feedback?

## Bottom line

This week’s evidence **supports and refines** the hypothesis more than it challenges it. The durable version should avoid claiming identity between human working memory and ant pheromone behavior. A stronger, evidence-weighted formulation is:

> Working memory and pheromone-guided collective behavior are different implementations of a broader directive-salience pattern: transient, feedback-sensitive states that make some actions more available than others. Consciousness may depend on whether such states are globally integrated/broadcast within an organism, not merely on whether they guide behavior.
