# Why Nations Fail

**Authors**: Daron Acemoglu, James A. Robinson  
**Year**: 2012  
**Citations**: ~9,100+ (cited heavily 2016–2026)  
**Venue**: Crown Business

## Key Contribution

Institutional theory of long-run growth: nations with "inclusive" institutions (broad political participation, secure property rights, pluralistic competition) prosper; those with "extractive" institutions (power concentrated, institutions designed for elites) stagnate. Institutions, not geography or culture, determine prosperity. History of critical junctures where institutional choice diverges.

## Intellectual Impact

Transformative for development economics and political economy. Cross-domain influence: history, political science, sociology, policy. Most-cited economics book of 2010s; shaped World Bank, IMF, and development NGO thinking.
