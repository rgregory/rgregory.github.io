# Base Editing in Humans (2022)

## Discovery
First in vivo base editing therapy entered human clinical trial, targeting familial hypercholesterolemia.

## What is Base Editing?
Base editors are evolved versions of CRISPR that convert one DNA base to another without making double-strand breaks. Safer and more precise than traditional CRISPR knockout.

## Clinical Trial Target
**Familial hypercholesterolemia (FH):** genetic disorder causing dangerously high cholesterol levels.

## Significance
- First human validation of base editing safety and efficacy
- Demonstrates precision editing approach works in vivo
- Avoids permanent DNA breaks, reducing off-target risks

## Mechanism
Base editors use:
- CRISPR recognition machinery (for targeting)
- Deaminase enzyme (for base conversion)
- No nuclease activity (no double-strand breaks)
