# Landmark Economics Papers (2016–2026)

## Overview

A curated collection of 20 landmark economics works **trending by citations across the 2016–2026 window**, organized by citation count and intellectual impact. Several foundational works (e.g. *Why Nations Fail* 2012, *Capital in the Twenty-First Century* 2013) were published earlier but accumulated the bulk of their citations and shaped the discourse during this decade. These papers represent the cutting edge of contemporary economic thought across inequality, technology, institutions, and macroeconomic theory.

> **Note on dates**: The 2016–2026 label reflects the *citation-trending window*, not publication dates. Actual publication span is 2012–2023 (see Statistics below).

## Intellectual Themes

**Recurrent themes across subfields:**
- **Inequality and distribution** — papers in all eight subfields address inequality from different angles (wealth, skills, markets, institutions)
- **Technological disruption** — labor & technology, macroeconomics, financial markets all grapple with digital transformation
- **Institutional foundations** — institutions shape growth, labor markets, and development outcomes
- **Behavioral realism** — move beyond rational-actor assumptions toward empirical human behavior
- **Measurement and methodology** — machine learning methods, well-being metrics, price indices as foundational epistemic tools
