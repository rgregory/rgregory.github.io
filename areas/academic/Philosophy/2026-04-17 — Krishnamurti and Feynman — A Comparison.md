# Krishnamurti and Feynman — A Comparison

## Research Prompt

> Understand Jiddu Krishnamurti and how Feynman compares.

---

## Jiddu Krishnamurti (to research)

- Indian philosopher and speaker (1895–1986); renounced the messianic role assigned to him by the Theosophical Society
- Central teaching: **the observer is the observed** — the psychological self is a construction of thought, and true freedom requires dissolving the thinker/thought division
- Critique of authority, tradition, and organized religion as obstacles to direct insight
- Emphasis on direct observation of the mind without a method — distinct from most contemplative traditions which prescribe technique
- Key works: *The Awakening of Intelligence*, *Freedom from the Known*, *The First and Last Freedom*

## Richard Feynman (connection to explore)

- Theoretical physicist (1918–1988); Nobel laureate; foundational work in QED, path integrals, Feynman diagrams
- Epistemological stance: radical empiricism; deep distrust of words, labels, and authority untethered from experiment
- Famous for distinguishing *knowing the name of something* from *knowing something* — a direct parallel to Krishnamurti's critique of verbal/conceptual thought
- Approach to learning: go back to first principles; question everything; don't trust received categories
- Connection to Krishnamurti: both were deeply skeptical of inherited authority; both emphasized direct encounter with reality over conceptual mediation; Feynman through physics, Krishnamurti through psychological self-inquiry

## Possible Comparisons

| Dimension | Krishnamurti | Feynman |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Domain | Psychological/spiritual | Physical/scientific |
| Method | Choiceless awareness; no technique | Experiment; derive from first principles |
| Attitude toward authority | Total rejection | Deep skepticism; show me the evidence |
| View of language/concepts | Words are traps; thought creates the problem | Names are not knowledge; math is the honest language |
| Goal | Freedom from the conditioned self | Understanding as intrinsic reward |
| Epistemology | Direct insight beyond thought | Empirical rigor; Bayesian updating |
