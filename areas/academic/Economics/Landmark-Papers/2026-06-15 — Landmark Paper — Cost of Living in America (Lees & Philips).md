# The Cost of Living in America: A Political History

**Authors**: Andrew Lees, Andrew Philips  
**Year**: 2016  
**Citations**: ~420+  
**Venue**: *Journal of Economic History*

## Key Contribution

Reconstructs real wages and cost-of-living baskets for US working-class households 1850–2000. Shows nominal wage growth masks stagnation in real purchasing power for many periods; cost inflation in housing, healthcare, education outpaced nominal wage growth. Links distributional outcomes to institutional choices and policy.

## Intellectual Impact

Historicized wage inequality debates; informed recent labor-history scholarship. Cross-citations in labor economics, public health (healthcare costs), education economics. Bridges economic history and contemporary policy.
