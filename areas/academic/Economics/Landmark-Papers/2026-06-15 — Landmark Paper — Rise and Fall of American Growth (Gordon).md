# The Rise and Fall of American Growth: The U.S. Since 1870

**Author**: Robert J. Gordon  
**Year**: 2016  
**Citations**: ~3,200+  
**Venue**: Princeton University Press / *American Economic Review*

## Key Contribution

Gordon's work advances the secular stagnation hypothesis, arguing that long-run productivity growth has decelerated sharply since the 1970s. Rather than viewing this as cyclical, he frames it as structural: the extraordinary productivity gains of 1870–1970 (electrification, automobiles, antibiotics, air travel) cannot be replicated by digital innovation. The book connects institutional dynamics, innovation cycles, and the mathematics of compound growth to explain why future growth may remain constrained.

## Intellectual Impact

Cross-domain influence spans macroeconomics, economic history, technology studies, and policy. Challenges Silicon Valley's technological optimism with rigorous historical analysis. Feeds into debates on inequality, stagnation, and the viability of perpetual growth models.
