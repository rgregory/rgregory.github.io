# Western Buddhism and Insight Meditation Teachers

A comment from an online discussion highlighting three teachers who are often overlooked in popular accounts of Buddhism's spread to the West.

## Key Figures

**Jack Kornfield**, **Sharon Salzberg**, and **Joseph Goldstein** are the founding forces behind the **Insight Meditation Society (IMS)** in Barre, Massachusetts — arguably the most influential institution in the popularization of Buddhism in the Western world. Kornfield also founded **Spirit Rock Meditation Center** in California, which draws tens of thousands of visitors each year.

Their shared lineage is the **New Burmese Method** of Insight Meditation (Vipassana), rooted in the teachings of **Mahasi Sayadaw**.

## Recommended Resources

For anyone exploring this tradition further:

- **Joseph Goldstein** — podcasts (widely available)
- **Mahasi Sayadaw** — primary texts on Vipassana technique
- **Jack Kornfield** — introductory texts (accessible entry points for Western readers)
- **Bhikkhu Bodhi** — translations and scholarly commentary (described as essential reading across the board)

## Context

The commenter notes five years of deep engagement with this tradition and describes the Insight Meditation approach as "life changing." The observation is made in the context of a piece that omitted these teachers despite their outsized influence.
