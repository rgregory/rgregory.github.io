# Pan-Respiratory Nasal Vaccine — Single-Dose Coverage (2025)

In 2025, research teams reported progress on **intranasal pan-respiratory vaccines** — single-dose formulations designed to provide mucosal immunity against multiple respiratory pathogens simultaneously: COVID-19, influenza, and RSV at minimum, with some candidates extending to additional respiratory viruses.

---

## The Mucosal Immunity Rationale

Standard intramuscular vaccines generate strong systemic immunity (IgG antibodies in blood, memory T and B cells). They are effective at preventing severe disease and death. What they do less well is prevent **infection at the mucosal surface** — the nasal passages and upper respiratory tract where respiratory viruses first establish themselves.

**Secretory IgA (sIgA)**, the dominant immunoglobulin in mucosal secretions, can neutralize pathogens before they reach the lower respiratory tract. Generating sIgA requires immunization at the mucosal surface itself — which intranasal delivery achieves. This is why a nasal vaccine, in principle, can block transmission and upper respiratory symptoms that injectable vaccines miss.

---

## The Pan-Respiratory Approach

Rather than separate vaccines for COVID, flu, and RSV, the pan-respiratory concept packages antigens from multiple pathogens in a single formulation:

- **Spike protein** or conserved epitopes from SARS-CoV-2 variants
- **Hemagglutinin/neuraminidase** from influenza A and B (or conserved stalk domains for broader coverage)
- **Prefusion RSV-F protein** (the structural biology insight from the RSV vaccine work)

The challenge is immunodominance and tolerance — how to ensure the immune response is appropriately distributed across all antigens rather than focusing on the most immunogenic one. Adjuvant selection and antigen ratios are key design variables.

---

## 2025 Research Advances

Multiple research groups and companies reported preclinical and early Phase 1 results in 2025:

- Intranasal formulations showing sterilizing immunity against all three target pathogens in animal models
- Some candidates using **virus-like particles (VLPs)** displaying multiple antigens; others using **mRNA-LNP formulations** adapted for mucosal delivery
- First human safety data from early Phase 1 trials showing acceptable tolerability and measurable sIgA induction
- The "universal flu vaccine" work (targeting conserved HA stalk) provides an anchor for the flu component

---

## Significance

If a single annual or biennial intranasal dose can cover COVID variants, flu strains, and RSV, the implications are large:

- **Compliance**: one dose instead of three separate vaccinations
- **Upper respiratory protection**: mucosal immunity reduces transmission, symptomatic infection, and long COVID/long respiratory illness risk — not just severe disease
- **Speed of deployment**: a pan-respiratory platform could be updated for new variants with antigen swaps, similar to annual flu vaccine reformulation
- **Healthcare burden**: respiratory infections account for a disproportionate share of primary care visits, lost workdays, and antibiotic overprescription globally

---

## Challenges Remaining

- Mucosal delivery of mRNA and protein antigens is harder than intramuscular delivery — stability, uptake, and adjuvantation are all more complex
- Regulatory pathways for novel mucosal adjuvants are less established
- Coverage breadth (how many strains/variants) vs. depth (magnitude of protection) tradeoffs are not yet resolved
- Animal model sIgA responses do not always translate to humans
