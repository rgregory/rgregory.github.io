# Capital in the Twenty-First Century

**Author**: Thomas Piketty  
**Year**: 2013 (English translation 2014)  
**Citations**: ~8,500+  
**Venue**: Belknap Press

## Key Contribution

Piketty's central mechanism: when the rate of return on capital (r) exceeds the rate of economic growth (g), wealth concentration accelerates indefinitely. Using 300 years of tax and wealth data, he documents the rise of inequality pre-1900, compression mid-20th century, and resurgence post-1980. The book proposes global wealth taxation as a remedy and frames inequality as a political choice, not a law of capitalism.

## Intellectual Impact

Transcends economics into sociology, political philosophy, and public discourse. Reshaped global inequality conversation. Cited across law, philosophy, environmental studies (resource concentration), and development economics. Controversial but generative — sparked decade of high-profile responses and refinements.
