# Fish City: Ecosystem Near Nuclear Plant on Lake Huron

Scientists in Ontario discovered a massive "fish city" thriving in warm waters near a nuclear plant on Lake Huron. Thousands of fish have gathered in a unique ecosystem — striking example of nature's resilience and adaptation to thermal conditions.

## Notes
- Thermal discharge from nuclear operations creates microhabitat
- Species concentration suggests optimal conditions for fish aggregation
- Demonstrates ecological benefits of thermal plumes in certain contexts
- Potential research opportunity for understanding thermal ecology
