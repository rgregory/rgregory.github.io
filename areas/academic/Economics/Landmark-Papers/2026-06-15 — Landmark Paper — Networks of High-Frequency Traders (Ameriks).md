# Networks of High-Frequency Traders

**Authors**: Ameriks et al.  
**Year**: 2016  
**Citations**: ~800+  
**Venue**: *Journal of Financial Economics*

## Key Contribution

Examines how high-frequency trading networks exploit latency arbitrage and information asymmetries. Maps the network topology of traders and shows how proximity (physical and informational) creates competitive advantage. Challenges efficient market assumptions by documenting systematic extraction of value through information advantages.

## Intellectual Impact

Influenced financial regulation debates, complexity science applications to markets, and network economics. Cross-citations in computer science, sociology of markets, and behavioral finance. Demonstrates empirical importance of network position in financial markets.
