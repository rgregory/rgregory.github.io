# Biocomputing

<!-- Stub note — expand when ready -->

Biocomputing is the use of biological systems — cells, proteins, DNA, and living tissue — as computational substrates. Unlike silicon computing, biocomputing leverages the inherent information-processing machinery of biology: protein folding, gene regulation, neural circuits, and molecular signalling.

## Key Questions
- What can biological systems compute that silicon cannot (and vice versa)?
- What are the energy efficiency tradeoffs of biological vs. electronic computation?
- How does DNA computing (Adleman, 1994) relate to classical complexity theory?
- How do engineered gene circuits (synthetic biology) function as logic gates?
