Agent memory is the missing software layer.

The opportunity is not a larger vector store. It is an evidence ledger that preserves where a memory came from, when it was true, who may use it, what superseded it, and why it entered an agent’s context.

Recommended direction
Build provenance infrastructure, not another agent framework.

Append observations once. Keep inference, search, graphs, profiles, and prompt context as replaceable projections.

01 / Why it matters

Intelligence without continuity is a demo.

A frontier model without durable memory repeatedly starts over. A frontier model with careless memory becomes confidently stale, contaminated, invasive, or impossible to audit.

Memory determines an agent’s continuity, identity, learning, coordination, economics, and trust. The critical problem is not simply what to recall, but what authority recalled data should have.

01

Omission

A decisive fact or commitment never enters context.

02

Contamination

A malicious or low-authority source becomes agent belief.

03

Staleness

A superseded fact continues to govern action.

04

Authority collapse

Quotation, paraphrase, and inference become indistinguishable.

05

Scope leakage

One person, project, or tenant sees another’s memory.

06

Privacy overreach

Temporary or sensitive data persists without control.

07

Irreproducibility

No one can explain why a memory shaped a decision.

Evidence before inference

An observation records what a source said or what occurred. An inference records what a model or person concluded. They may be retrieved together; they must never become indistinguishable.

Forgetting is part of memory

Correction, supersession, permission revocation, expiry, and deletion are lifecycle operations—not an account-level afterthought.

03 / Conceptual design

One journal. Small programs. Disposable projections.

The source of truth is an immutable, namespaced event ledger. Search indexes, vectors, graphs, summaries, profiles, and prompt context are derived products—not privileged memory.

Hosted boundary

PostgreSQL provides concurrency, row-level security, jobs, full-text search and pgvector. The transactional outbox replaces an early message broker.

Local boundary

SQLite WAL, FTS and optional embeddings implement the same command contract for a single user, offline research, tests and export.

# one record in; one result out
printf '%s\n' '{"schema":"memory.v1","subject":"project:p7", \
  "kind":"observation","text":"Award report due 30 Sep", \
  "source":{"ref":"award.pdf#page=12"},"idempotency_key":"award:12:3"}' \
| mem-normalize \
| mem-put

printf '%s\n' '{"schema":"search.v1","query":"reporting obligations", \
  "as_of":"2026-07-15T00:00:00Z","limit":10}' \
| mem-query

04 / Market wedges

Sell an outcome, not “memory.”

The defensible wedges are narrow systems of record for recurring obligations: show how an interpretation formed, prove which witness supports a reading, remember what a narrator allowed, or demonstrate why a grant commitment was fulfilled.

Commercial entry

Grant commitments and closeout

Clear buyer, recurring deadlines, measurable audit and handoff labor, and an obligation-to-evidence schema.

Product lighthouse

Philosophy argument provenance

Forces exact citation, epistemic types, objections, contested claims, editions and longitudinal reasoning.

Trust showcase

Rights-aware oral history

Makes permission inheritance, revocation, cultural protocol and derivative use the product itself.

05 / Go to market

Start where memory has an owner and a deadline.

Individual faculty are valuable design partners but weak initial economic buyers. Research offices, centers, libraries, archives and accreditation programs can own a bounded workflow and budget.

Positioning

  • Category: provenance infrastructure for AI agents.
  • Promise: Memory your agents can cite, correct, and delete.
  • Enemy: opaque context without source, authority, revision, permission or expiry.
  • Value metric: active governed workflow or obligation portfolio—not tokens.

Initial offer

  • 12-week fixed-scope obligation-memory pilot.
  • 25–50 grants, one center or sponsor.
  • Read-only source intake; human-approved obligations.
  • Baseline and outcome report, export bundle and verified deletion path.
  • Pricing hypothesis: $15k–$30k; validate during discovery.

Distribution

  • Research-administration associations and NIH-heavy centers.
  • APA, humanities institutes and research librarians for the lighthouse.
  • OHA and SAA workshops for the governance showcase.
  • Open local CLI plus Zotero, Obsidian and MCP adapters.

Procurement baseline

  • HECVAT, data-flow map and dated SOC 2 roadmap.
  • SSO, tenant isolation, audit export and no-training terms.
  • Retention, legal hold, deletion, export and offboarding.
  • WCAG 2.2 AA target and NIST AI RMF mapping.
Days 1–30

Problem proof

Fifteen interviews, three workflow maps, baseline labor and buyer/budget validation.

Days 31–60

Product proof

Journal, temporal revisions, provenance retrieval, export and revocation evaluation.

Days 61–90

Buyer proof

One paid design partner or two signed pilots; stop if praise never becomes ownership and budget.
