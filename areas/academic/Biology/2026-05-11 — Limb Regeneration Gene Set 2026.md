# Limb Regeneration Gene Set (2026)

## Discovery
Researchers studying axolotls, zebrafish, and mice identified a shared set of genes that may enable limb regeneration across species.

## Model Organisms
- **Axolotl:** naturally regenerates limbs (salamander)
- **Zebrafish:** naturally regenerates fins and other structures
- **Mice:** normally do NOT regenerate limbs (mammalian model)

## Key Finding
Despite evolutionary distance and different regenerative abilities, all three species share conserved genes governing limb regeneration.

## Significance
- Identifies molecular basis for regeneration capacity
- Suggests regeneration genes are ancient and conserved
- Implies mammalian regeneration may be "turned off," not "absent"
- Opens path to therapeutic regeneration in humans

## Mechanism (Hypothesis)
- Shared developmental program for limb patterning and growth
- Differences may lie in:
  - Gene regulation/timing
  - Tissue inflammation control
  - Scarring suppression
  - Stem cell mobilization

## Clinical Potential
Understanding regeneration genes could enable:
- Limb regeneration in mammals (including humans)
- Tissue engineering scaffolds using regeneration signals
- Therapeutic activation of dormant regeneration pathways
