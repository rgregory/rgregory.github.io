# Printed Skin Engineering (2024)

## Discovery
Scientists successfully 3D-printed multilayered skin constructs using six types of human skin cells, achieving scarless regeneration in animal models.

## Key Components
Six cell types printed in biomimetic layers:
- Epidermal cells
- Dermal cells
- Fibroblasts
- Hair follicle stem cells
- Sebaceous gland cells
- Vascular cells

## Biological Outcomes
- Enhanced re-epithelialization (wound closure)
- Reduced contraction (prevents contracture scars)
- Minimized scarring
- Full-thickness skin with complex structures

## Structures Regenerated
- Epidermis
- Dermis
- Hair follicles
- Sebaceous glands
- Vasculature

## Testing
- Small animal models (positive results)
- Large animal models (positive results)
- Demonstrates scalability toward human application

## Clinical Promise
- Burns and trauma wounds
- Chronic wounds
- Aesthetic reconstruction
- Functional recovery of skin appendages
