# Debt, Deleveraging, and Macroeconomic Performance

**Authors**: Kenneth Rogoff, Carmen Reinhart  
**Year**: 2015  
**Citations**: ~2,200+  
**Venue**: *American Economic Review*

## Key Contribution

Empirical study of debt accumulation and deleveraging episodes using long historical dataset. Documents patterns: high debt (>90% of GDP) correlates with slower growth; deleveraging is slow and painful, typically taking 5-10 years; financial crises are recurrent and costly.

## Intellectual Impact

Influential in austerity debates post-2008; shaped policy arguments for and against debt reduction. Controversial (famous errors/critiques) but widely cited. Cross-domain: cited in policy circles, development economics, history of financial crises.
