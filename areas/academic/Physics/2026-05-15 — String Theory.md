# String Theory

## The Core Idea

String theory replaces the point particles of standard quantum field theory with one-dimensional objects — strings — whose length is on the order of the Planck length (~10⁻³⁵ m). The different vibrational modes of a single string correspond to different particles: one object, many manifestations. A string vibrating in a certain mode is an electron; in another mode, a quark; in yet another, a graviton.

## Why Strings?

The deepest motivation is a mathematical one: point particles and gravity don't coexist gracefully in quantum field theory. When you try to quantize general relativity using standard QFT techniques, you get non-renormalizable infinities — divergences that cannot be absorbed into a finite number of counterterms. The theory breaks down at the Planck scale.

Strings solve this by spreading interactions over a finite length. Rather than two particles colliding at a single spacetime point (the source of the infinities), two strings interact over an extended worldsheet, which smooths out the divergences. String theory is, to date, the only known consistent quantum theory that includes gravity.

## Historical Arc

- **1968** — Veneziano amplitude: Gabriele Veneziano writes down a formula for strong-force scattering that is later recognized as describing strings.
- **1970s** — Nambu, Goto, and Polyakov develop the action principles for bosonic strings. The theory requires 26 dimensions for consistency — clearly too many, and bosonic strings have a tachyon instability.
- **1984 — First superstring revolution**: Green and Schwarz show that anomaly cancellation in superstring theory (incorporating supersymmetry) works in 10 dimensions, and only in 10 dimensions. Five consistent superstring theories emerge (Type I, IIA, IIB, Heterotic SO(32), Heterotic E₈×E₈).
- **1995 — Second superstring revolution**: Witten argues that all five 10D string theories are limits of a single 11-dimensional framework, M-theory. Web of dualities (T-duality, S-duality) relates theories that look entirely different at weak coupling.
- **1997 — AdS/CFT**: Maldacena proposes that string theory on anti-de Sitter space is exactly dual to a conformal field theory on its boundary. The holographic correspondence becomes the most productive tool in theoretical physics of the following decades.
- **2000s–present — Landscape and swampland**: the realization that ~10⁵⁰⁰ distinct vacua are consistent with string theory motivates the landscape program; the swampland program attempts to identify which low-energy effective theories cannot be UV-completed by string theory.

## Key Features

### Extra Dimensions
Superstring theory requires 10 dimensions (9 spatial + 1 time) for mathematical consistency. We observe only 4. The remaining 6 are compactified — curled up at Planck-scale radii, invisible to current experiments. Their geometry determines what particle physics looks like in the 4D effective theory. The most studied compactification geometries are **Calabi-Yau manifolds** — complex 3-dimensional spaces with special holonomy that preserve supersymmetry in 4D.

### Supersymmetry (SUSY)
Superstring theory requires supersymmetry: every boson has a fermionic superpartner (and vice versa). SUSY is also theoretically attractive for stabilizing the Higgs mass hierarchy. However, no superpartners have been observed at the LHC, pushing the scale of SUSY breaking (if it exists) well above the TeV range.

### Open vs Closed Strings
- **Closed strings**: loops with no endpoints. The graviton is always a closed string mode — which is why gravity appears in any string theory.
- **Open strings**: have endpoints that are constrained to live on **D-branes** (Dirichlet branes) — dynamical surfaces in the higher-dimensional spacetime. Gauge fields live on D-branes. This D-brane picture is central to model-building and to the AdS/CFT construction.

### Dualities
- **T-duality**: a string theory compactified on a circle of radius R is equivalent to one compactified on radius α′/R (where α′ is the string tension). Small and large radii are physically identical.
- **S-duality**: strong coupling in one string theory maps to weak coupling in another. This makes the web of string theories non-perturbatively consistent.

### M-Theory
The 11-dimensional framework that unifies all five 10D string theories as different weakly-coupled limits. The fundamental objects are not strings but M2-branes and M5-branes. The 11th dimension is a line segment (Hořava-Witten) or a circle, and compactification on different manifolds reproduces the five string theories. M-theory has no known Lagrangian formulation.

## The Landscape Problem

Compactifying the extra six dimensions on different Calabi-Yau manifolds, with different flux configurations, gives a vast number of distinct 4D effective theories — the **string landscape**, estimated at ~10⁵⁰⁰ vacua. Each vacuum has different particle content, coupling constants, and cosmological constant. The question "which vacuum is ours?" has no clean answer from first principles.

This prompted Susskind and others to invoke the **anthropic landscape**: we live in a vacuum compatible with observers, and eternal inflation populates all vacua in a multiverse. Critics regard this as abandoning the predictive aspirations of fundamental physics.

The **swampland program** (Vafa, 2005–) takes a different approach: characterize which effective field theories are inconsistent with a UV completion in quantum gravity. Swampland conjectures (de Sitter conjecture, distance conjecture, weak gravity conjecture) constrain the landscape and make contact with observation — e.g., the de Sitter conjecture constrains slow-roll inflation models.

## Experimental Status

No direct experimental evidence for string theory exists. The string scale is of order the Planck length (~10⁻³⁵ m), some 15 orders of magnitude below what the LHC can probe. Potential indirect signatures:

- **Cosmic strings**: macroscopic string-like defects that could leave imprints in gravitational wave backgrounds or CMB non-Gaussianities.
- **Gravitational wave signatures**: certain brane cosmology models predict distinctive GW spectra.
- **Swampland constraints**: if swampland conjectures are correct, they rule out certain inflationary models that future CMB experiments could test.
- **SUSY at the LHC**: absence of superpartners continues to constrain string-inspired models.

## Criticisms

- **Unfalsifiability**: no prediction unique to string theory has been confirmed. The landscape makes it arguably impossible to falsify in the Popperian sense (Smolin, Woit).
- **Too many vacua**: 10⁵⁰⁰ choices is not a prediction — it is the abdication of one.
- **SUSY not found**: the simplest string-inspired models predicted SUSY at the TeV scale; the LHC found nothing.
- **No unique prediction**: the theory has not converged on a standard model of particle physics derivation.

**Defenders** argue: AdS/CFT is rigorously established and has generated real results in condensed matter and nuclear physics (quark-gluon plasma). String theory is the only UV-complete quantum gravity we have. Mathematical consistency is a constraint, not a failure.
