# Immune-Neural Circuit (2024)

## Discovery
Researchers identified a neural circuit that directly regulates immune system function.

## Significance
Reveals bidirectional communication between nervous and immune systems at the circuit level, not just through hormones or general signaling.

## Mechanism
The neural circuit:
- Directly innervates immune organs (spleen, lymph nodes, bone marrow)
- Modulates immune cell activation and proliferation
- Coordinates inflammatory responses
- May suppress excessive inflammation

## Biological Context
Brain-immune interactions previously understood through:
- Neuroendocrine signals (HPA axis)
- Cytokine signaling (systemic)
- Local neuroinflammation

This discovery adds direct **neural control** pathway.

## Clinical Implications
Potential therapeutic targets for:
- Autoimmune diseases (excessive immunity)
- Immunosuppression (insufficient immunity)
- Chronic inflammation
- Sepsis and acute inflammation

## Research Directions
- Map full immune-neural connectivity
- Identify circuit-specific functions
- Develop circuit-targeted interventions
- Explore parallel circuits in mammals/humans
