# Dementia Differential — LBD vs. VaD vs. AD

A synthesis note bridging the three most common dementias. The clinical problem: they overlap heavily (**mixed dementia is 40–50% of cases at autopsy**), yet getting the distinction right changes treatment — most acutely the **antipsychotic contraindication in LBD**, where a single dose can be fatal in ~50% of patients.

Bridges: [[2026-06-23 — Lewy Body Dementia]] · [[2026-06-23 — Vascular Dementia]] · [[2026-06-23 — Alzheimer's Disease]].

---

## The One-Line Decision Rules

- **AD** → insidious, *episodic-memory-led*, rapid forgetting **not** rescued by cueing; clean DaTSCAN, **amyloid-positive** biomarkers, hippocampal atrophy on MRI.
- **LBD** → **visual hallucinations + RBD + parkinsonism + cognitive fluctuations**; clean structural MRI but **abnormal DaTSCAN**; catastrophic antipsychotic sensitivity.
- **VaD** → **stepwise or executive/processing-speed-led** decline + vascular risk factors; **infarcts / white-matter disease on MRI**, normal DaTSCAN; the most *preventable* and most *modifiable* of the three.

---

## Master Comparison Table

| Dimension | **Alzheimer's (AD)** | **Lewy Body (LBD)** | **Vascular (VaD)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary pathology** | Amyloid-β plaques + tau tangles | Alpha-synuclein (Lewy bodies), prion-like spread | Cerebrovascular injury (infarcts, white-matter ischemia) |
| **Onset** | Insidious | Insidious | Often abrupt (post-stroke) or stepwise |
| **Course** | Gradual, steady, relentless | Steady **with fluctuating attention/alertness** | **Stepwise** (MID) or gradual (small-vessel) |
| **Hallmark early deficit** | Episodic memory | Visuospatial + attention/executive; fluctuation | Executive dysfunction + slowed processing |
| **Memory failure type** | **Storage** — cueing does NOT help (rapid forgetting) | Relatively spared early | **Retrieval** — cueing helps |
| **Visual hallucinations** | Uncommon early (~10–20% late) | **~80%, early, vivid, formed (core)** | Uncommon unless mixed |
| **REM sleep behavior disorder** | Rare | **Core feature; often precedes by 10–15 yrs** | Not characteristic |
| **Cognitive fluctuations** | Absent | **Core feature** | Possible with events |
| **Parkinsonism / motor** | Late/advanced only | **Early, bilateral (core)** | Gait apraxia / focal signs (vascular, not parkinsonian) |
| **Other early signs** | Anomia, visuospatial late | Autonomic dysfunction, hyposmia | Focal neuro signs, pseudobulbar affect, early urinary/gait |

## The Imaging & Biomarker Split (the decisive part)

| Test | **AD** | **LBD** | **VaD** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Structural MRI** | Hippocampal / medial temporal atrophy; **low** white-matter burden | Relatively **normal**; preserved hippocampus; no infarct burden | **Abnormal** — infarcts, lacunes, **WMH (high Fazekas)**, microbleeds |
| **DaTSCAN** (dopamine transporter SPECT) | **Normal** | **Abnormal** (reduced striatal uptake) | **Normal** |
| **MIBG cardiac scintigraphy** | Normal | **Low uptake** (sympathetic denervation) | Normal |
| **Amyloid PET** | **Positive** | May be positive (co-pathology) | Negative unless mixed |
| **CSF** | **Low Aβ42, low Aβ42/Aβ40, high p-tau181/total-tau** | AD markers may be abnormal; **α-synuclein SAA positive** | Nonspecific |
| **Plasma biomarker** | **p-tau217** (high accuracy), p-tau181 | α-synuclein SAA (emerging) | NfL, endothelial markers (research) |
| **Polysomnography** | — | **Confirms RBD** (>98% specific for synucleinopathy) | — |

> **The cleanest single discriminator: DaTSCAN.** Abnormal → LBD. Normal → AD or VaD (then structural MRI separates those two: atrophy pattern = AD, vascular lesions = VaD).

## Diagnostic Criteria Sets

| | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **AD** | NIA-AA 2011 (clinical) / 2018 ATN research framework / 2024 biological revision; DSM-5 Major/Mild NCD due to AD |
| **LBD** | 4th DLB Consortium Consensus Criteria (2017): core features + indicative biomarkers |
| **VaD** | NINDS-AIREN (1993, specific/insensitive) / VASCOG (2014) / DSM-5 Vascular NCD; requires cognitive deficit + cerebrovascular disease + a **temporal/causal link** |

## Treatment Divergence

| | **AD** | **LBD** | **VaD** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cholinesterase inhibitors** | Responsive (modest) | **Strong response** (cholinergic deficit worse than AD) | Modest/uncertain; use if mixed AD suspected |
| **Memantine** | Moderate–severe AD | Modest | Limited |
| **Disease-modifying drugs** | **Lecanemab** (~27% slowing, CLARITY-AD), **donanemab** (~35%, TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2) — anti-amyloid mAbs; require amyloid confirmation; ARIA risk | None | None — *but* risk-factor control modifies course |
| **Antipsychotics** | Mild–moderate caution (elderly mortality/stroke risk) | **SEVERE contraindication** — ~50% neuroleptic hypersensitivity, can be fatal; use pimavanserin/quetiapine only if unavoidable | General caution |
| **Disease-specific lever** | Anti-amyloid therapy in early disease | RBD/prodromal detection window; avoid neuroleptics | **Vascular prevention** — BP, glucose, statins, AF anticoagulation (most preventable dementia) |

## Epidemiology & Prognosis

| | **AD** | **LBD** | **VaD** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **% of all dementia** | ~60–70% (most common) | 2nd most common *degenerative* (~1.4M US) | ~15–20% (2nd most common overall) |
| **Mean survival from dx** | ~8–10 yrs | ~5–8 yrs | ~3–5 yrs |
| **Peak onset** | Rises sharply >65 | 70–85 | Rises steeply with age |

---

## The Two Traps to Avoid

1. **Satisfaction of search.** Mixed dementia is 40–50% at autopsy. Finding white-matter disease does NOT exclude LBD; finding amyloid does NOT exclude a vascular contribution. Stopping at the first pathology is the classic error — see [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Health-Systems/2026-04-04 — Differential Diagnosis Reasoning Under Uncertainty|Differential Diagnosis — Reasoning Under Uncertainty]].
2. **The antipsychotic reflex.** Early prominent visual hallucinations should push you *toward* LBD and *away from* reaching for a neuroleptic. In LBD this is the difference between symptom control and a fatal reaction — optimize a cholinesterase inhibitor first.
