# Fruit Fly Connectome (2024)

## Discovery
First complete connectome of an entire fruit fly (*Drosophila melanogaster*) brain mapped in full detail.

## Scale
- **139,000+ neurons**
- **50 million synaptic connections**
- Complete wiring diagram at synaptic resolution

## Methodology
Advanced electron microscopy (EM) with:
- Serial-section transmission EM (ssEM)
- Automated image segmentation
- Machine learning-assisted neuron tracing
- Manual annotation and verification

## Biological Significance
- Drosophila brain is a model for understanding general neural principles
- ~100,000 neurons is small enough for complete mapping, large enough for complex behavior
- Every neuron and connection now visible as starting point for functional analysis

## Research Potential
Enables studies of:
- Neural circuits for learning and memory
- Sensory processing (vision, smell, taste)
- Motor control
- Decision-making
- How structure relates to behavior

## Broader Impact
Proof of concept for connectome-level neuroscience; establishes methods scalable toward larger brains and species.
