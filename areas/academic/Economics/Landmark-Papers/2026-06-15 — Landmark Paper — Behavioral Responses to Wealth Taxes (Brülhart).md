# Behavioral Responses to Wealth Taxes: Evidence from Switzerland

**Authors**: Marius Brülhart et al.  
**Year**: 2016  
**Citations**: ~620+  
**Venue**: *Journal of Human Resources*

## Key Contribution

Exploits cantonal variation in Swiss wealth tax rates to estimate tax elasticity of mobility. Finds high-net-worth individuals respond strongly to tax changes via migration, not just behavioral avoidance. Wealth tax incidence falls on remaining residents through reduced capital stock and public services.

## Intellectual Impact

Shaped wealth tax debates in policy circles (notably Piketty's influence). Cross-domain: cited in public finance, migration studies, behavioral economics. Informed arguments against global wealth taxes by highlighting mobility/sovereignty trade-offs.
