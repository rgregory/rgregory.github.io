# Clinical Acronyms — Internal Medicine Reference

A working reference for IM residents. Organized by clinical domain.

---

## Assessment Frameworks

**ABCDE** — Primary survey (any unstable patient)
Airway → Breathing → Circulation → Disability (neuro/GCS) → Exposure

**SAMPLE** — History taking
Symptoms, Allergies, Medications, Past medical history, Last meal, Events leading up

**OLDCARTS / SOCRATES** — Pain history
Onset, Location, Duration, Character, Aggravating factors, Relieving factors, Timing, Severity
*(SOCRATES: Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associations, Time course, Exacerbating/relieving, Severity)*

**AVPU** — Rapid consciousness assessment
Alert, Voice (responds to), Pain (responds to), Unresponsive

---

## Altered Mental Status / Delirium

**AEIOU-TIPS** — Causes of AMS
Alcohol, Epilepsy, Insulin (glucose), Opioids, Uremia; Trauma, Infection, Psychiatric, Stroke/Structural

**CAM** — Confusion Assessment Method (delirium diagnosis)
1. Acute onset + fluctuating course
2. Inattention
3. Disorganized thinking
4. Altered level of consciousness
*(CAM-positive = criteria 1 + 2 + either 3 or 4)*

**I WATCH DEATH** — Delirium etiology (comprehensive)
Infection, Withdrawal, Acute metabolic, Trauma, CNS pathology, Hypoxia, Deficiencies (B12/thiamine), Endocrine, Acute vascular, Toxins/drugs, Heavy metals

---

## Metabolic / Acid-Base

**MUDPILES** — Elevated anion gap metabolic acidosis
Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, Isoniazid/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates

**HARDASS** — Normal anion gap (hyperchloremic) metabolic acidosis
Hyperalimentation, Addison's disease, RTA, Diarrhea, Acetazolamide, Spironolactone, Saline infusion

---

## Cardiology

**CHADSVASC** — Stroke risk in AFib (anticoagulation threshold ≥2)
Congestive HF (1), Hypertension (1), Age ≥75 (2), Diabetes (1), Stroke/TIA (2), Vascular disease (1), Age 65–74 (1), Sex (female = 1)

**HAS-BLED** — Bleeding risk with anticoagulation
Hypertension (uncontrolled), Abnormal renal/liver function, Stroke history, Bleeding history/predisposition, Labile INR, Elderly (>65), Drugs (NSAIDs/antiplatelets) or alcohol

---

## Sepsis / Critical Care

**qSOFA** — Bedside sepsis screening (≥2 = high risk)
Altered mental status, Respiratory rate ≥22, Systolic BP ≤100

**SIRS** — Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (≥2 criteria)
Temperature >38°C or <36°C, HR >90, RR >20 or PaCO2 <32 mmHg, WBC >12k or <4k or >10% bands

**DOPES** — Ventilated patient sudden deterioration
Displacement (ETT), Obstruction, Pneumothorax, Equipment failure, Stacking (auto-PEEP)

---

## Toxicology

**SLUDGE** — Cholinergic toxidrome (organophosphate/nerve agent)
Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI distress, Emesis

**DUMBELS** — Cholinergic (expanded)
Diarrhea, Urination, Miosis, Bradycardia, Emesis, Lacrimation, Salivation

*(Anticholinergic is the reverse: "Mad as a hatter, hot as Hades, blind as a bat, dry as a bone, red as a beet, full as a flask")*

---

## Neurology / Stroke

**BE-FAST** — Stroke recognition (expanded from FAST)
Balance, Eyes, Face, Arm, Speech, Time to call 911

**ROSIER** — Recognition of Stroke in the Emergency Room
Scoring tool: facial weakness, arm weakness, leg weakness, speech disturbance, visual field defect (positive); seizure activity, loss of consciousness (negative points)

---

## Infectious Disease

**ESKAPE** — Priority resistant pathogens
Enterococcus faecium, Staphylococcus aureus, Klebsiella pneumoniae, Acinetobacter baumannii, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Enterobacter spp.
*(The organisms that "escape" standard antibiotic coverage)*

---

## Electrolytes

**C BIG K Drop** — Hyperkalemia treatment sequence
Calcium (membrane stabilization), Bicarb, Insulin + Glucose, Kayexalate/Patiromer, Dialysis

---

## Rheumatology / MSK

**GALS** — Rapid musculoskeletal screen
Gait, Arms, Legs, Spine

---

## Wound Care / Chronic Disease

**TIME** — Wound bed preparation framework
Tissue (non-viable), Infection/Inflammation, Moisture imbalance, Edge (non-advancing)
*(Also used in stroke recognition in some curricula — context-dependent)*
