# Rockefeller 2024 Vitamin A Study — Study Details

## Publication

- **Journal**: *Science*
- **Year**: 2024
- **Volume / Issue**: 383(6687)
- **DOI**: [10.1126/science.adi7342](https://www.science.org/doi/abs/10.1126/science.adi7342)
- **Published**: March 7, 2024

## Institution

**Rockefeller University**, New York. The research group is affiliated with Rockefeller's Laboratory of Mammalian Cell Biology and Development (Elaine Fuchs lab — one of the foremost stem cell and skin biology groups globally).

## Research Question

Does retinoic acid (vitamin A metabolite) regulate the duration and depth of lineage plasticity in hair follicle stem cells during skin wound repair?

## Model System

Mouse skin wound repair model. Hair follicle stem cells (HFSCs) were the primary cell population under study. Cells were traced using genetic lineage markers to track identity transitions in vivo across the wound repair timeline.

## Experimental Interventions

The study used three distinct strategies to manipulate RA signaling, providing convergent causal evidence:

### 1. Genetic Intervention
- Conditional knockout of RA signaling components specifically within hair follicle stem cells
- Allowed cell-autonomous dissection of RA's role without systemic confounds
- Result: HFSCs failed to re-commit to follicle identity after wound closure; extended plastic state

### 2. Dietary Intervention
- Systemic vitamin A deficiency induced by vitamin A-depleted diet
- Depletes the precursor pool for RA biosynthesis throughout the animal
- Result: mirrored genetic knockout phenotype — prolonged plasticity, impaired follicle reconstitution

### 3. Topical Intervention
- Exogenous retinoic acid applied directly to wound sites
- Pharmacological gain-of-function complement to the loss-of-function genetic/dietary approaches
- Result: accelerated exit from plasticity and earlier re-commitment to follicle identity

## Key Findings Summary

| Condition | HFSC Behavior | Wound Outcome |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Normal RA signaling | Returns to follicle identity after repair | Normal skin reconstitution |
| RA signaling blocked (genetic) | Stays in plastic state | Impaired follicle architecture |
| Vitamin A deficiency (dietary) | Stays in plastic state | Impaired follicle architecture |
| Exogenous RA applied (topical) | Accelerated re-commitment | Faster follicle reconstitution |

## Conceptual Contribution

The study introduces the **rheostat model** of lineage plasticity control: RA does not function as a binary switch but as a graded signal whose level determines the degree of stem cell commitment. This is mechanistically distinct from prior models that treated plasticity as an all-or-nothing state.

## Significance in Field

- First demonstration that a diffusible small molecule (RA) directly controls the *exit* from lineage plasticity in adult tissue stem cells
- Connects dietary vitamin A status to stem cell identity dynamics — with implications for wound healing, regenerative medicine, and cancer biology
- Validates pharmacological modulation of plasticity as a feasible therapeutic strategy
