# Liesegang Rings

Pattern formation in chemical systems. Self-organized precipitation patterns in gels and solutions — a canonical example of weak emergence where simple local reactions (diffusion + precipitation) produce complex global periodic banding without central coordination.

## Key Insight

Liesegang rings instantiate the core emergence principle: simple rules at the molecular scale → complex ordered structures at the macro scale. No external blueprint, no central director. This is emergence operating in the domain of inorganic chemistry.
