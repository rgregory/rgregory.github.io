# Explosive Evolution Model (2025)

## Discovery
New mathematical model reveals that evolution proceeds in explosive bursts rather than gradual, continuous change.

## Theoretical Framework
Challenges the classical **gradualism** model (slow, steady accumulation of mutations) with evidence of:
- Rapid adaptive radiation periods
- Long stasis (no change) periods
- Concentrated change events

## Model Dynamics
- **Stasis phase:** species relatively stable morphologically
- **Burst phase:** rapid speciation and morphological change
- **Drivers:** environmental pressure, niche availability, genetic innovation

## Evolutionary Mechanisms
- Punctuated equilibrium (already proposed by Eldredge & Gould 1972)
- New math formalizes mechanism and predicts when bursts occur
- Suggests evolution is non-random in **timing**, not just **direction**

## Evidence Support
- Fossil record shows abrupt appearances of new species
- Speciation in real-time (cichlid fish, Darwin's finches)
- Adaptive radiation after mass extinctions

## Implications
- Evolution is not a smooth gradient but a rhythm
- Rapid change possible when conditions align
- May explain Cambrian Explosion and other radiation events
- Suggests evolutionary rates are predictable under specific conditions
