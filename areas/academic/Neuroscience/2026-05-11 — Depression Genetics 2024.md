# Depression Genetics (2024)

## Discovery
Large genome-wide association study (GWAS) identified 50+ new genetic loci and 205 novel genes associated with major depressive disorder.

## Study Design
- 21 studies analyzed
- Nearly 1 million participants
- Largest depression genetics study to date

## Key Findings
**New genetic associations:**
- 50+ genomic loci (chromosomal regions)
- 205 novel genes implicated in depression risk
- Many genes previously unknown to play a role

## Biological Pathways Identified
Genes point to molecular pathways involved in:
- Synaptic transmission
- Neurogenesis (birth of new neurons)
- Neuroinflammation
- Neuroendocrine signaling
- Circadian rhythm regulation

## Clinical Significance
- Moves depression beyond "monoamine hypothesis"
- Reveals biological heterogeneity (multiple pathways to depression)
- Identifies new drug targets
- Enables precision medicine approaches (genetic risk stratification)

## Drug Development Impact
New therapeutic avenues beyond:
- SSRIs (serotonin reuptake inhibitors)
- SNRIs (norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors)
- Potential for pathway-specific interventions
