# Monopsony Power in Higher Education

**Authors**: David Laband, Bernard Lentz  
**Year**: 2012  
**Citations**: ~450+  
**Venue**: *Journal of Labor Economics*

## Key Contribution

Analyzes institutional labor-market power in academic labor markets. Shows universities exploit monopsony power (limited job mobility for academics) to suppress wages relative to productivity. Financial aid policies subsidize institution salaries at the cost of wage inequality among faculty.

## Intellectual Impact

Opened institutional critique of labor markets beyond textbook competition. Influenced subsequent work on wage-setting power, labor market concentration, and monopsony resurgence in modern economies.
