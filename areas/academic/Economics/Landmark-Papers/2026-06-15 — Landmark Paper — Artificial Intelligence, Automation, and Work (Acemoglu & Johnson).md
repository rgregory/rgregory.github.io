# Artificial Intelligence, Automation, and Work

**Authors**: Daron Acemoglu, Simon Johnson  
**Year**: 2018–2023  
**Citations**: ~900+  
**Venue**: Multiple papers and books

## Key Contribution

Distinguishes "automation" (replacing labor) from "complementary technology" (augmenting labor). Argues much recent AI is task-specific automation with limited complementarities, risking mass displacement. Proposes policy interventions: reskilling, task-specific innovation, potentially robot taxation.

## Intellectual Impact

Reshaped AI-labor debate from determinism to agency/policy. Cross-domain: cited in AI ethics, labor economics, technology policy, sociology. Informs regulation and public discourse around AI deployment.
