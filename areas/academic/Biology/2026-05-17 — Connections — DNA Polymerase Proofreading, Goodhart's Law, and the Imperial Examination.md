# Connections — DNA Polymerase Proofreading, Goodhart's Law, and the Imperial Examination

## The Problem

How do you enforce fidelity between a proxy and an underlying goal when only the proxy is measurable?

Three systems face this problem and find different answers.

## DNA Polymerase Proofreading

DNA pol's 3'→5' exonuclease domain physically re-examines the just-inserted nucleotide — the verifier operates on the same substrate as the primary action, with no interested party between them. Error rate drops from 1 in 10^5 to 1 in 10^9.

This works because the goal (correct base pairing) and the proxy (Watson-Crick geometry) are the same thing at the molecular level. There is no gap between "what we can measure" and "what we actually want."

## The Imperial Examination (Keju)

Moral character and practical wisdom (zhì, rén) are not physically re-examinable the way base pairs are. The examiner cannot access the goal directly — only the proxy (essay performance) is available. Under 500 years of optimization pressure, the proxy decoupled completely from the goal.

The Eight-Legged Essay became a pure formal exercise — candidates optimized for the proxy without any mechanism forcing alignment with the underlying quality the system was designed to select for.

## Goodhart's Law as the General Principle

Any measure under optimization pressure ceases to be a good measure. The keju is its longest-running historical case study (500 years). The molecular proofreading system shows what Goodhart-resistance requires: a verifier operating on the goal itself, not on a proxy.

Governance systems structurally cannot achieve this because the goals of governance (wisdom, judgment, character) are tacit and opaque in exactly the sense Hayek's Knowledge Problem describes.

## The RNA Polymerase Contrast

RNA pol *deliberately* omits proofreading because its outputs are transient and abundant — errors don't compound. The keju made the opposite assumption: examination results were treated as irreversible signals of permanent character.

High-stakes irreversibility plus proxy measurement is Goodhart's Law's most favorable environment.

## The Deep Structure

| System | Can verifier access goal directly? | Stakes | Goodhart outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| DNA polymerase | Yes (same substrate) | Existential (genome integrity) | Solved — 10^9 fidelity |
| RNA polymerase | No (but doesn't need to) | Low (transient outputs) | Avoided — errors are cheap |
| Imperial Examination | No (character is opaque) | High (career, dynasty) | Full Goodhart failure over 500 years |
| Modern standardized testing | No | High | Running the same experiment now |
