# The Prion Information Paradox

## The Biology

Prions are misfolded proteins that induce normal proteins of the same type to misfold in an identical way. The misfolded conformation propagates across cells, across individuals, and — in some cases — across species. Prion diseases include CJD (Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease), scrapie, and BSE (mad cow disease).

What makes prions extraordinary is that they carry no nucleic acid — no DNA, no RNA. Every established mechanism of biological inheritance and information transfer depends on nucleic acids. Prions violate this without exception.

## The Paradox

If information is defined as a pattern that can be copied and transmitted with fidelity, then prions are unambiguously transmitting information. The specific misfolded three-dimensional shape propagates reliably — not randomly. Yet there is no known informational molecule doing the encoding.

The implied answer is that the protein conformation itself is the information carrier. **Shape is the information.**

This is paradoxical because molecular biology's standard account of information flow — the Central Dogma — treats sequence as the fundamental informational currency. DNA encodes sequence; sequence determines structure; structure determines function. Prions suggest the causal arrow can run the other way: structure can propagate itself, independent of sequence.

## Why It Matters Philosophically

**1. It challenges the Central Dogma as a complete account of biological information.**
The Central Dogma (DNA → RNA → protein) describes one well-understood channel of information flow. Prions demonstrate that this channel is not the only one. The formal system does not account for all the information-carrying phenomena within its own domain — a situation structurally similar to Gödel's result: truths that exceed what the system's own rules can generate. See [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Philosophy/2026-03-21 — Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems|Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems]].

**2. It forces a broader definition of biological information.**
A sequence-centric definition of biological information is too narrow. Prions demand a definition that includes stable, self-propagating physical structures regardless of their molecular substrate. This connects to questions in the philosophy of information: is information substrate-independent? Can any physical pattern that reliably copies itself count as information, regardless of mechanism?

**3. It implicates emergence and levels of description.**
The prion's misfolded conformation is a property of the whole three-dimensional protein — not deducible from the amino acid sequence alone (same sequence, two stable conformations). The information is emergent in the strong sense: it resides at a level of organization that is not reducible to the lower-level description. This is the Aggregation Problem applied to molecular biology: the level of description (sequence) misses properties that only appear at a higher level (conformation). See [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Philosophy/2026-03-22 — The Aggregation Problem|The Aggregation Problem]].

**4. It blurs the boundary between information processing and physical structure.**
If shape can carry and transmit information without a genetic template, then the distinction between "information processor" and "physical structure" becomes unstable. This has direct implications for the philosophy of mind: functionalist and computationalist accounts typically assume information is substrate-independent and abstractable from its physical instantiation. Prions suggest the opposite case — that some information is entirely constituted by a specific physical configuration and cannot be abstracted from it.

## The Unresolved Tension

The mechanism remains incompletely understood. Something selects for the misfolded conformation over the normal one when the two come into contact. But what constrains that selection in the absence of a genetic template? The normal protein already contains the misfolded shape as a possible conformation — the prion does not introduce new sequence information, only a new stable state. Why does contact with the misfolded form tip the balance?

That gap — between "the information is clearly propagating" and "we don't know what is doing the selecting" — is where the paradox lives. It is not just a gap in current knowledge; it may require a genuinely new account of what biological information is.
