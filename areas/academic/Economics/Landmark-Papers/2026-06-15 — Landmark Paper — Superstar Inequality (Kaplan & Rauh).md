# Superstar Inequality

**Authors**: Steven N. Kaplan, Joshua D. Rauh  
**Year**: 2013  
**Citations**: ~680+  
**Venue**: *Brookings Papers on Economic Activity*

## Key Contribution

Documents rise of "superstar" inequality: extreme top-income concentration among executives, athletes, entertainers, professionals. Shows mechanism combines skill, market size, and compensation choices. Executive pay growth outpaces productivity; cultural/sports/media earnings highly concentrated.

## Intellectual Impact

Shaped top-income inequality discourse; challenged technological skill-premium explanations with institutional/market-power narratives. Cross-citations in labor, executive compensation, inequality studies.
