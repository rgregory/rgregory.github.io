# Hericium erinaceus — Lion's Mane

## Basic Facts

- **Family**: Hericiaceae
- **Common names**: Lion's mane, bearded tooth mushroom, monkey head mushroom (China: 猴头菇)
- **Distribution**: North America, Europe, Asia; grows on hardwood (oak, maple, walnut, beech)
- **Ecology**: Saprophytic and weakly parasitic — decomposes dead wood, occasionally infects living trees
- **Culinary**: Edible; flavor profile compared to crab or lobster; widely cultivated

## The Mechanism — Why This One Matters

The fruiting body produces **hericenones** (aromatic compounds); the mycelium produces **erinacines** (diterpenes). Both compound classes stimulate synthesis of **Nerve Growth Factor (NGF)** — a protein critical for the growth, maintenance, and survival of neurons.

What makes this unusual: erinacines cross the blood-brain barrier. Most NGF-stimulating compounds do not. This is the basis for the neuroscience research interest.

## Research Status

- **Animal studies**: Consistent NGF upregulation, especially in the hippocampus; improved memory and learning markers
- **Human trials**: Small, mostly Japanese; most-cited is Mori et al. (2009) — 30 subjects, mild cognitive impairment, 16-week double-blind RCT; significant improvement on cognitive function scale vs. placebo; effect reversed after discontinuation
- **Active research areas**: Alzheimer's disease, peripheral neuropathy, depression/anxiety, post-COVID neurological symptoms
- **Caveat**: Evidence base is early-stage; effect sizes in human trials are modest; most mechanistic work is in vitro or rodent models

- **Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptors** — [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Neuroscience/2026-05-16 — Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptors|nAChRs]] are the downstream target of the cholinergic neurons whose growth and survival NGF (stimulated by Lion's Mane) supports; the two mechanisms are complementary — NGF sustains the neurons, nAChRs are the receptors those neurons act on. The Alzheimer's disease angle links both notes: cholinergic neuron loss is the pathological substrate that NGF supplementation and AChE inhibition (galantamine is also an nAChR PAM) each address from a different angle.
- [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Health-Systems/Neurology/2026-06-23 — Alzheimer's Disease|Alzheimer's Disease]] — the primary disease target in Lion's Mane research: NGF stimulation supports the cholinergic neurons of the nucleus basalis of Meynert whose degeneration defines the cholinergic hypothesis of AD and is the mechanistic rationale for AChE inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine). The Mori et al. (2009) trial enrolled patients with mild cognitive impairment, the prodromal AD stage; the effect reversal on discontinuation mirrors the symptomatic (non-disease-modifying) profile of standard AD pharmacotherapy.
- [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Health-Systems/Neurology/2026-06-23 — Lewy Body Dementia|Lewy Body Dementia]] — LBD's cholinergic deficit is more severe than Alzheimer's, making the nucleus basalis of Meynert a critical target; the cognitive decline DDx context this note already invokes (Mori et al. excluded reversible causes) must include LBD as a differential; NGF-mediated cholinergic neuron support has the same rationale in LBD that it does in Alzheimer's
- [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Health-Systems/Neurology/2026-06-23 — Vascular Dementia|Vascular Dementia]] — in the very common mixed dementia presentation (VaD + AD, ~40–50% of dementia cases at autopsy), there is an AD/cholinergic component that NGF support rationale applies to; VaD must also be included in the cognitive-decline DDx workup the Mori et al. trial depends on — before attributing cognitive impairment to a condition amenable to Lion's Mane, stepwise vascular etiology must be excluded; the preventability of vascular cognitive impairment (blood pressure, statins, AF anticoagulation) makes the DDx distinction clinically high-stakes
- [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Biology/2026-05-16 — Endophytic Fungi|Endophytic Fungi]] — hericenones/erinacines as an instance of the broader pattern: endophytic and related fungi produce secondary metabolites with unexpected biomedical relevance; the same bioprospecting logic that found *Taxomyces andreanae* producing Taxol applies to Lion's Mane — organisms living inside or alongside living hosts evolve compounds under selection pressures that happen to produce pharmacologically active chemistry
