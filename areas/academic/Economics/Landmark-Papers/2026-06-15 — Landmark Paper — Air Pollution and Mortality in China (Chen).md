# The Effect of Air Pollution on Mortality in China

**Authors**: Chen et al.  
**Year**: 2013  
**Citations**: ~2,100+  
**Venue**: *Journal of Environmental Economics and Management*

## Key Contribution

Uses regression discontinuity design around China's Huai River heating policy to estimate causal effects of air pollution on mortality. Finds sharp gradient: northern cities (heated, more polluted) show significantly higher mortality than southern counterparts. Quantifies the health-cost shadow price of development and environmental externality.

## Intellectual Impact

Exemplary work in applied causal inference, environmental health economics, and development-environment trade-offs. Frequently cited in climate economics, public health, and policy circles. Demonstrates how natural experiments yield credible causal estimates in large populations.
