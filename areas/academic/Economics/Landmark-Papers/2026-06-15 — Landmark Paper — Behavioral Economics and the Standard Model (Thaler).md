# Behavioral Economics and the Standard Economic Model

**Author**: Richard H. Thaler  
**Year**: 2016+  
**Citations**: ~1,100+  
**Venue**: *Journal of Economic Literature* (handbook chapter)

## Key Contribution

Thaler's synthesis of behavioral economics: humans exhibit systematic departures from rational choice theory (bounded rationality, loss aversion, present bias, mental accounting). These are not anomalies but predictable patterns with policy implications. Argues for incorporating behavioral insights into economic models and policy design.

## Intellectual Impact

Nobel Prize-winning work (2017); shaped entire subfield. Cross-domain: cited in psychology, policy, business, neuroscience. Transformed how economists think about human behavior and design (nudge theory, choice architecture).
