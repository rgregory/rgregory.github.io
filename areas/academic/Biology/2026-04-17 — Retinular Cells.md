# Retinular Cells

**Retinular cells** are the photoreceptor neurons within each [[2026-04-04 — Ommatidia Units of the Compound Eye|ommatidium]] of an arthropod compound eye. Each ommatidium typically contains 6–9 retinular cells arranged around a central axis. They are the functional equivalent of the rod and cone cells in vertebrate retinae — but with a fundamentally different transduction architecture.

---

## Structure

Each retinular cell extends a **rhabdomere** — a tightly packed stack of microvilli containing the photopigment (typically rhodopsin). The microvilli are oriented perpendicular to the light path; this regular microvillar structure is what confers **polarized light sensitivity**, a capability vertebrate rods and cones lack entirely.

In flies, the 6 outer retinular cells (R1–R6) are broadband and feed into the motion-detection pathway. Two central cells (R7, R8) are spectrally distinct and feed into the colour/UV pathway.

---

## Rhabdom Types

| Type | Structure | Found in |
|---|---|---|
| Fused rhabdom | All rhabdomeres fused into one central rhabdom | Most insects |
| Open rhabdom | Rhabdomeres separate, each cell isolated | Higher Diptera (flies) |

Open rhabdom + neural superposition (in flies) = the resolution-preserving, sensitivity-boosting strategy unique to higher Diptera.

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **[[2026-04-04 — Ommatidia Units of the Compound Eye|Ommatidia]]** — structural parent; retinular cells are one of the core components
- **[[2026-04-17 — Apposition Eyes|Apposition Eyes]]** — in apposition design, each retinular cell samples only its own ommatidium's narrow field
- **[[2026-04-17 — Superposition Eyes|Superposition Eyes]]** — in superposition design, retinular cells receive summed input from many facets
- **[[2026-04-04 — Aristae Fly Acoustic Receiver|Aristae]]** — mechanosensory parallel: Johnston's organ hair cells transduce mechanical vibration as retinular cells transduce light — both are sensory transducers adapted to a specific physical modality
- **[[2026-04-17 — Philosophy of Mind|Philosophy of Mind]]** — the rhabdomere's polarization sensitivity means arthropod qualia (if any) include a dimension of visual experience — polarization direction — that is simply absent from human phenomenology

- **[[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Biology/2026-04-06 — The Square-Cube Law|The Square-Cube Law]]** — rhabdomere function depends on microvillar surface area packing photopigment; this is a surface-area-dependent transduction mechanism that scales favorably only at small body sizes; at vertebrate scales the microvillar architecture is replaced by membrane disc stacking in rods and cones — a different solution to the same surface-area constraint
