# CRISPR Gene Therapy Approved (2023)

## Discovery
**Casgevy** became the first FDA-approved CRISPR/Cas9-based gene therapy in late 2023, marking the transition from promise to clinical reality.

## Target Conditions
- Sickle cell disease
- Transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia

## Significance
Gene editing moved from laboratory to clinic. Casgevy uses CRISPR to edit patient hematopoietic stem cells, allowing them to produce functional hemoglobin and reduce transfusion dependence.

## Clinical Impact
Patients with these conditions previously had limited options beyond blood transfusions and supportive care. CRISPR therapy offers potential for functional cure.
