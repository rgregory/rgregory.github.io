# Molecular Computation

<!-- Stub note — expand when ready -->

Molecular computation is computation performed by molecules — particularly DNA, RNA, and proteins. Unlike electronic computers, molecular computers operate through chemical binding, conformational change, and reaction kinetics rather than electrical signals.

## Key Questions
- DNA computing (Adleman, 1994): how was the Hamiltonian path problem solved in a test tube?
- What are molecular logic gates and how do they implement Boolean logic?
- How does CRISPR function as a programmable molecular computer?
- What are the theoretical limits of molecular computation?
