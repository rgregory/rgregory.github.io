# Anthropology — Human Origins, Culture, Evolution

Map of content for the Anthropology self-study area: human origins, prehistory, archaeology, biological anthropology, and material culture. The area sits at the intersection of biology, history, and philosophy of science — anchored by case studies that make the methods of inference from physical evidence concrete.

---

## Case Studies

- [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Anthropology/Ötzi the Iceman — Neolithic Mummy of the Alps|Ötzi the Iceman]] — 5,300-year-old Alpine mummy; landmark Neolithic-to-Bronze-Age specimen; first fully sequenced ancient human genome (2012); forensic, dietary, and migratory reconstruction

## Neolithic Transition

*The shift from foraging to farming, sedentism, early metallurgy, and emergent social complexity.* Notes to develop:

- Ötzi sits exactly at this transition (copper axe + flint toolkit)
- Anatolian farmer expansion into Europe (genetic + archaeological evidence)
- Domestication packages: animals, cereals, settlement patterns

## Alpine Archaeology

*High-altitude prehistoric Europe: preservation conditions, mobility patterns, conflict.* Notes to develop:

- Glacial preservation as a methodological lens (Ötzi as the type case)
- Trans-Alpine trade and migration in late Neolithic / early Bronze Age
- Pastoralism vs. settled agriculture in mountain ecologies

## aDNA / Genomics

*Ancient DNA as an evidentiary revolution in anthropology.* Notes to develop:

- The 2010s aDNA boom: methods, contamination controls, enrichment
- Three-population model of European ancestry (WHG / EEF / Steppe)
- Ötzi's genome as proof-of-concept and reference point
- Cross-link: [[02-Areas/Learning/Self-Study/Biology/CRISPR|CRISPR]] and adjacent molecular methodology
